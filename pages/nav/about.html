Why we exist

The web is full of tutorials that show you what to type. We write about why it works, when to use it, and what you're trading away when you choose one approach over another. That distinction matters more than most people realise when they're starting out.

We founded The Craft because we kept seeing the same pattern: developers who could build things but couldn't explain them. Designers who could make things look good but couldn't articulate why they made the decisions they did. Those are the gaps we write into.

"The goal is always the same: leave the reader with a mental model they can apply to the next problem, not just a solution they can copy for this one."

Every article we publish goes through the same question: does this make the reader permanently better at their work? If the answer is yes, we publish it. If the answer is "they'll use this once and forget it", we don't.

We are not sponsored. We do not run ads. We do not accept payment for coverage. The only thing that funds The Craft is people who find it valuable enough to subscribe to the newsletter and tell their colleagues about it.

What we believe

Depth over volume

One well-researched article is worth more than ten quick takes. We publish once a week, never daily.

Honest opinions

We say when something is overrated. We say when we changed our minds. We don't write hype.

Respect for your time

Every article is as long as it needs to be and no longer. We cut ruthlessly in editing.

Written by practitioners

Every author on The Craft writes code or ships designs professionally. No content farms.

Accessibility matters

Every page we publish meets WCAG 2.2 AA. We eat our own cooking.

Independent always

No investors, no sponsors, no affiliate links. Our only obligation is to the reader.

Write for The Craft

If you build things professionally and have something genuine to say about the craft of doing it well, we want to hear from you. We don't accept pitches about tools, products, or services. We accept pitches about ideas, techniques, and hard-won lessons.

We pay contributors a flat fee per article and give full editorial support — a real editor, not a chatbot.
